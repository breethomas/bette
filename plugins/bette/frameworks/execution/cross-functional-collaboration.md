# Cross-Functional Collaboration

How PMs work across teams when you have responsibility but not authority.

---

## DACI

- **Driver:** Owns the work and the timeline. Usually the PM.
- **Approver:** One person who makes the final call. NOT a committee. If two people can veto, nobody can decide.
- **Contributors:** Provide input, expertise, options. Their job is to make the Approver smarter.
- **Informed:** Need to know the outcome. Do not need to shape it.

The most common failure: too many Approvers. Every additional Approver multiplies decision time and dilutes accountability. If you cannot name one Approver, you have a governance problem, not a collaboration problem.

Write the DACI down. Share it at kickoff. Refer back to it when roles blur.

---

## Influence Without Authority

You cannot mandate cooperation. You earn it.

- **Understand their incentives.** What are they measured on? What does success look like for them? Start there.
- **Speak their language.** Engineers care about technical quality and feasibility. Sales cares about pipeline and quota. Finance cares about unit economics. Translate your ask.
- **Make the ask easy.** Do the homework. Come with a specific request, not an open-ended problem. Reduce the effort they need to spend.
- **Show the upside for their goals.** "This helps you because..." beats "I need this because..." every time.

Influence compounds. People help people who have helped them and who make their lives easier.

## Working With Specific Functions

**Engineering:** Bring context, not solutions. Explain the problem and the constraints. Trust their estimates but challenge scope -- "what if we shipped half of this?" Respect technical debt conversations; they are protecting your future velocity.

**Design:** Align on the problem before exploring solutions. Share user evidence -- quotes, data, observed behavior. Opinions without evidence waste design cycles.

**Data/BI:** Define the question before requesting analysis. Share what you will do with the answer -- it changes how they build the query. Vague asks produce vague results.

**Marketing:** Give them the "why" and the "who." Let them own the "how." They know their channels better than you do.

**Sales:** Listen to their objections. They are relaying customer reactions in real time. Share enough roadmap context for them to sell honestly, not so much that unshipped features become promises.

---

## Alignment Rituals

Fewer, shorter, more focused beats more meetings.

- **Weekly with eng:** Status, blockers, decisions. 30 min max.
- **Bi-weekly with stakeholders:** Progress against goals, upcoming decisions, input needed. Not a demo -- demos are separate.
- **Quarterly with leadership:** Strategy check. Are we still building the right thing?

Every recurring meeting should have a clear purpose sentence. If you cannot state it, cancel the meeting.

---

## Failure Modes

**Bikeshedding.** Trivial decisions consume disproportionate time because everyone has an opinion and nobody has stakes. Fix: set a decision deadline and a single Approver.

**Diffusion of responsibility.** "We all own this" means nobody owns it. Fix: name one Driver. One.

**Consensus trap.** Waiting for everyone to agree before moving. Consensus feels safe but optimizes for the least objectionable option. Fix: use DACI. The Approver decides, Contributors advise. Disagreement is fine. Blocking is not.
